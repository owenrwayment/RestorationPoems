Restoration Timeline

Some major events in the early history of the church.

  • Spring of 1820

    The First Vision

    A young boy named Joseph Smith sought for truth among many different opinions. In repsonse to his earnest prayer, God the Father and His Son Jesus Christ appeared to Joseph. His sins were forgiven and He was told that the fulness of truth was lost from the earth but would shortly return.

  • September 1823

    Appearance Of Angel Moroni

    Once again God answered Joseph Smith's prayers and sent him an angel named Moroni. Moroni instructed Joseph that God had a work for him to do and showed him the location of an ancient record called the Book of Mormon, another testament of Jesus Christ.

  • April 1830

    Organization of the Church of Christ

    Joseph was commanded to organize Christ's New Testament church on the earth once more. Roughly 40 people were gathered in the Whitmer home to witness God's marvelous work continue to unfold.

  • April 1836

    Restoration of Keys

    After the completion of the temple in Kirtland, Oliver Cowdery and Joseph Smith were visited by the Savior, Elijah, Moses and Elias. They were given the authority and responsibility to bring God's children unto Him, both the living and the dead.

Vision

A poem filled with metaphors about God speaking once again.

Noisy
A band without director stumbles through the notes they remember
Unsure
Pretending to know the tune of Heaven
Ruckus
Each peformer plays louder than the other

Passion
A hireling plays with coals from fire
Danger
Flames lick others, boiling their blood, singeing their eyes
Blinded
Sightlessly leading others to the same fate

Digger
Not content with the silly surface soil
Searching
For gems of true value hidden to human eye
Guidance
Ancient dust directs from worn pages

Quiet
Away from the noise and boiling blood
Kneeling
Rooted to the ground, seeks succor from above
Evil
Smoke gathers to choke the young sapling

Open
A new sun rips wide the clouded air
Fire
Engulfs the trees with light and warmth
Spirit
Fills the forest, fills the boy, fills what was missing

Answers
Graciously bestowed gifts, their contents to bless the world
Spoken
Not wrapped but breathed from two persons
Restored
Rusty yokes renewed, eternal bonds recovered

The Little Blue Book

Written in San Diego, CA on a personal witness of the book.

The little blue book
I gave it a look
It told a story
One of great glory
About a Savior to come

A prophet of old
By the Lord was told
To take a far trip
And build a big ship
They went to the promised land

Brothers torn by pride
After father died
Two nations were made
Much conflict did trade
For hundreds of years they fought

Until a day and night
Where the land was light
A sign of Christ's birth
That He'd come to earth
So then the people rejoiced

And when Christ had died
Three days did go by
With darkness so thick
No fire was lit
The people cried out in fear

Then they heard Christ speak
His message unique
Full of love and peace
The dark cloud did cease
They felt the prints in His hands

At the end of all
A promise does call
That those who will read
And fall to their knees
Will come to know that it's true

Knowledge I lacked
So I chose to act
I prayed and I read
As the Spirit led
To show me it all was right

A little blue book
Was all that it took
For me to find out
Without any doubt
That Christ both lives and loves

A note on the Law of Consecration

A poem that highlights the challenges of consecrating our lives.

My dear,
What will we do?
Bend, bow, mold and grow?
Or
Stiffen, harden, lock, and wither?
Choice apparent
Not easily made

Naturally,
Land is mine, house is mine, money is mine
Bought in sweat, cracked hands, long days
Mine
To give like a river is to run dry
Unless we too are replenished
Mountain water promised

My heart,
Eager to beat its own rhythm
Like stone, set in its own way
But
The master stonemason comes
Reshapes this rough, rocky road inside me
To a smooth path paved with gold

So,
As the clouds give forth all their mostiure
So will I give everything
And
Town, mind, heart, soul become one
A shared goal, a common vison
The same God guides us

Consecrate,
Not an offering left on the altar
But an axe employed
Best
Heart, might, mind, strength
Operating for the Good Shepherd
Who multiplies my loaves and fishes

With love and whole soul,
His steward

Kirtland Keys

A poetic recap of Doctrine and Covenants section 110.

A temple opened
The world filled with light
An ensign erected
Our story to rewrite

Praying in his house
There were two earnest men
A vison received
After their last amen

There on the pulpit
Stood our Redeemer Christ
Pleased with their labor
In Him, it was sufficed

His eyes were fire
His head was fallen snow
Brighter than the sun
His rushing voice did flow

Then came three persons
With power to restore
Moses, to gather
And bring Israel once more

Then came Elias
With covenants of old
To bless God's children
Through promises untold

Lastly Elijah
He came to save the dead
Save us from a curse
Like Malachi had said

Again, keys given
Authority received
The work goes forward
And it will be achieved

Brother Joseph, Martyr

A sad summary of the death of the first prophet of this dispensation.

A promise of five years
Comes to a close with tears.

Four men in a warm cell
Soon to see men from hell.

John, singing a sweet song
Twice sung couldn't be wrong

Noise outside, black faces grin
Death determined to win

Shots ringing, brace the door
See Hyrum on the floor

Scramble to the window
Death behind, death below

"Oh Lord, my God" he cried
Bullets pierced his back side

Now we sing, praise the man
For his work in this land
